# Scientific Contributions

## Pioneer and long-term contributor to AI-based transportation modeling
Car-following and lane-changing models are cornerstones for understanding driver behavior, traffic dynamics, and supporting autonomous driving. As early as 2014, at the dawn of the deep learning era, my team proposed an innovative kNN-based data-driven car-following model. This model has been cited almost 200 times, and it is widely recognized as one of the first data-driven car-following models by review articles published by scholars from institutions such as the University of California, Berkeley (Zhang et al., 2025), the University of Washington (Chen et al., 2023), and Imperial College London (Rowam et al., 2025).

In 2019, my team developed the first deep-learning-based lane-changing model using DBN and LSTM neural networks, which has since been cited over 300 times and is widely recognized in subsequent research as a pioneering contribution to AI-driven modeling of driver behavior and traffic dynamics.

Prediction is a central topic in transportation research and practice. I contributed to the development of the first CNN-based network-wide traffic speed prediction model, and the single paper has been cited over 1,600 times. Other representative works include spatial-temporal graph attention transformer-based trajectory prediction, graph CNN-based bike-sharing demand prediction, GAN-based travel time prediction, etc. All these works were published in leading journals, such as TRC, CACAIE, and Information Fusion, and have received substantial citations.

## An early innovator in large language model (LLM) applications in transportation
Since the emergence of LLMs in 2023, my team was among the first to introduce LLM-based intelligent driving assistance, highlight the positive role of prompt engineering in enhancing prediction accuracy, and develop AI agent models for everyday route choice, with a series of early papers published in leading journals.

These works rank among the earliest applications of LLMs and have attracted wide attention in the research community. In 2024, I was twice invited to introduce those LLM-based progresses in ground mobility at SAE International, the world’s leading authority on automotive industry standards. At the beginning of 2026, we had a Correspondence, titled *AI writing tools could lead scholars from low-income countries to erase their own voices*, which was published in Nature.

## Developed autonomous driving strategies to mitigate traffic congestion
To address highway congestion and emissions, my team was the first to propose a feedback control–based congestion mitigation strategy for connected vehicles and the jam-absorption driving strategy, with results published in IEEE TITS and more than 300 citations and reported by Italian media.

Researchers in Japan cited our work on jam-absorption strategies more than ten times in a single paper, recognizing the two-step jam-absorption strategy as an effective means of alleviating traffic congestion. I am leading a survey paper co-authored with scholars from MIT, Georgia Tech, and TU Delft, which is forthcoming in IEEE TITS.

For the urban scenarios, I introduced a novel future no-lane-change road transportation system for autonomous driving and subsequently extended the framework to include pedestrians. The series of works was published in IEEE TIV, IEEE TVT, and TRC, drawing wide attention from the research community.

I have also devoted significant effort to predicting the motion of surrounding agents for autonomous vehicles, with a particular focus on intersection conflict scenarios. To date, I have developed a series of methods, including approaches based on Transformers, LLMs with CoT reasoning, and World Models.

## Advanced traffic state sensing and visualization
Time-space traffic diagrams are one of the most important traffic analysis and visualization tools. The series of works centered on the enhancement of the diagrams, including the mapping-to-cell approaches, the wave-aware construction, the traffic emission estimation and the resolution enhancement.

In particular, the wave-aware method of constructing time-space diagrams has been substantively implemented in the well-known I-24 MOTION project in Nashville, as well as in the works of global researchers from the UK, Sweden, and China. Notably, these implementations were carried out completely independently of our own research activities.

## Other Research Fields
I am also actively engaged in resilience, sustainability, shared mobility, and travel behavior research. Representative works cover transportation system vulnerability assessment, mesoscopic traffic emission modeling, multi-day travel behavior modeling and experimental route choice research.

I co-authored multiple highly cited review articles in transportation research, which have guided subsequent research and gained wide academic recognition.
